Did You Know?

This map highlights global tertiary education enrolment rates. Australia stands out with an enrolment rate of 112%, driven by its popularity as a destination for international students. Factors like high-quality education, diverse study options, and favorable immigration policies make countries like Australia an attractive choice.

Education and the Economy

Analysing how economic policies correlate with tertiary enrollment rates

Countries with higher GDP per capita usually have higher tertiary education enrollment. However, nations like Australia and Greece stand out by enrolling more students than their populations, reflecting their role as education hubs. Hover on the chart to explore further!

Wealthier countries, such as those in Scandinavia, generally spend more on education per person and tend to have higher tertiary enrollment rates. This suggests that increased investment in education often correlates with more people pursuing higher education. Hover on the chart to explore more!
