Fun Fact!

The US remains the top destination for tertiary education, attracting students from nearly all parts of the world due to its diverse range of prestigious institutions, research opportunities, and cultural exposure.

The UK and Australia are also becoming increasingly popular, particularly among students from Asia and Europe, owing to their world-class education systems, scholarship opportunities, and post-graduation work incentives.

Have You Noticed?

The number of international students in Malaysia saw consistent growth from 2007 to 2010, and from 2013 to 2016. This steady increase was driven by Malaysia's growing reputation as a cost-effective and accessible destination for tertiary education, especially among students from neighboring regions.

However, the sharp declines in 2017 and 2018 saw a drop of over 43,000 students, likely due to changes in government policies, competition from other education hubs, and global economic factors.

Education and the Economy

Analysing how economic policies correlate with tertiary enrollment rates

Countries with higher GDP per capita usually have higher tertiary education enrollment. However, nations like Australia and Greece stand out by enrolling more students than their populations, reflecting their role as education hubs.

Wealthier countries, such as Norway, generally spend more on education per person and tend to have higher tertiary enrollment rates. This suggests that increased investment in education often correlates with more people pursuing higher education.
